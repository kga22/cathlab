WELCOME!

This Interventional Catheterization Online Resource is intended to provide introductory education for staff new to the Cath Lab. It is by no means a comprehensive resource. As always, it is important to take a proactive approach during orientation and supplement with independent learning. This is only meant to provide a basic foundation of knowledge for those in training.

By nature, the cath lab is an ever evolving environment. Advances in technology and an increase in patient volume allow for its expansion. In tandem with this growth, is an evident shortage of nurses and the need to prepare new staff to be functional in the most efficient way.

This website aims to cover the basic concepts that will help gain understanding of what goes on in the cath lab. It is broken down into different sections, starting with EQUIPMENT. Being inside the procedure room requires knowledge of what the physician is asking for, and even anticipating what may be needed.

The other sections are self-explanatory. Listed are the main procedures performed, as well as short descriptions of miscellaneous devices implanted or placed in the lab. There is also a section of terminology with which the staff should be familiar, and useful links for more educational materials. References used to collect data for this online resource are scattered throughout the site, and the majority of it are what is found in the LINKS section.
